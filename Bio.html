I first became intrigued with technology when I first picked up a remote. I was amazed that by a click of a button a little plastic remote could change the channels on my television without physically being connected to it. This stimulated a thought process that until this point I never knew I had. I wanted to know how this worked but for years the answer eluded me in the depths I sought. I didn’t know much about life at this point but I knew mine would include technology.

After finishing my formal education at Rainier Beach High school, I went on to pursue higher education at Washington State University. This was one of the hardest challenges I ever faced in my life because not only was I facing an increased workload from an educational standpoint; I had to learn how to be responsible given the new-found freedom I had gained from leaving home. This coupled with the fact that I still wasn’t sure what exact career path I was passionate about caused me to struggle throughout my undergraduate career. This adversely affected my self-confidence for years. So much so that I would end up leaving before completing my last course to get my degree in Management Information Systems.

This would lead me back to Seattle to find work. The easiest jobs to obtain were manual labor jobs and so I would do just that. Thinking that the only valuable skill I had to offer was to be a laborer. I would do these simple jobs to make ends meet but I knew in my heart that I wasn’t happy with this type of work. I knew I could make a more valuable contribution to society, I just needed to find out how. Back in my Management Information Systems courses at Washington State I was introduced to programming, but never went into depth on the matter. I decided it was time to go back and give it a look. After a couple of months of confusing videos on YouTube I decided I needed a more structured learning environment, which lead me to invest in TeamTreehouse.com. One of the best decisions I’ve ever made.

Learning programming was nothing like I thought it would be. The syntax of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript was difficult for me to grasp. There were so many steps involved in rendering content that at times it was too much for me. I began to stumble in my life once again, so you can only imagine what this did for my confidence. For long periods I would leave code alone but I would always come back to it because I always found a sense of accomplishment when I finished projects. This persistence helped develop a consistent habit of putting in hours of studying code after I clocked off work. I finally felt what it was like to be passionate about something. I started to develop a more robust understanding of these concepts and now I can have an endless conversation about these languages, though I don’t advise that outside of a tech friendly audience. As my web development skill developed so did my confidence. I was still unsure of what my future had in store, but with these new skills I was eager to find out. I now had a new sense of worth.

In Retrospect I believe all parts of my journey were necessary for me to experience to bring me to the point I’m at today. Even though I saw them as failures previously each experience I had in my life taught me something different. Being a part of University level courses taught me that the amount of effort put into being prepared and educated on subjects that are vital to your work is correlated to the quality of the execution of that mentioned work. The jobs I had taught me how to better communicate and express my opinions to individuals in higher and lower positions than myself. The fact that it is going to take me well beyond 4 years to complete my degree has taught me that everyone’s journey goes at their own speed and you shouldn’t compare yourself to others. Building on that previous statement, I would like to mention I plan on completing my last class of my coursework this upcoming summer to complete my degree. I mention all of this because these lessons not only help me as a web developer but in all aspects of my life.

As of now I am a free-lance web developer pursuing opportunities and projects to utilize, showcase, and develop my programming acumen. Feel free to contact me or follow my social media accounts for continued updates on my journey.
Peace and Progress
