I first became intrigued with technology the minute I picked up a remote. The concept that by a click of a button a little plastic device could change the channels on my television blew my mind. I didnâ€™t know much about life at this point but I knew I wanted mine to include technology and that is where my journey to learn programming began.

As a kid I went to a elementary school called Rainier View in Seattle, WA. A big passion throughout my life has been basketball and back in grade school we would divide teams up by the portables we were assigned to. I was apart of the illustrious P5 squad!!!! My friend Julian made a web page for P5 that had the names of everyone in our group in a cool design. He never taught me how he made that page but I made it my mission to find out.

The funny thing is coding wouldn't be reintroduced to my life until the tail end of my time at Washington State University. I initially went to school with the intention of becoming an engineer but I soon realized I wasn't happy on that career path. I ended up switching into Management Information Systems which isn't directly based in coded but related. In classes they had us using tools to help up populate web page templates but I wanted to know things to a deeper level. This led me to employ methods to teach myself HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Learning how to code was nothing like I thought it would be. The syntax of the languages were difficult for me to grasp. There were many steps involved in performing simple task that at times I thought it was too much for me. Despite everything I maintained persistent with learning at my speed. I started to develop a more robust understanding of these concepts over time, which has increased my confidence as a developer. My plan is to continue to tackle new projects to grow my skillset.

In Retrospect I believe all parts of my journey were a necessary experience for me; in order to be where I'm at today. A web developer pursuing opportunities and projects to utilize, showcase, and develop my programming acumen. Feel free to contact me or follow my social media accounts for continued updates on my journey.
